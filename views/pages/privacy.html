Privacy Policy

Your data. Your journey. Applies to users in Ghana and abroad Last updated: 2025-01-01

This Privacy Policy explains how Elite Travel & Tour (“Elite”, “we”, “our”, “us”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects your information when you use our website, mobile experiences, Work & Pay programs, Express routes, visa services, flights, tours, and related products (collectively, the “Services”).

Plain-language summary: We collect only the information we genuinely need to run your journey, keep your account secure, and comply with legal and partner obligations. We do not sell your data.

1. Who this policy covers

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • Individuals using our public website, booking forms, tracking pages, and user dashboards.
  • Applicants and participants in Work & Pay, Express, visa, flight, tour, and orientation programs.
  • Visitors who interact with us via contact forms, support channels, and newsletter subscriptions.

By using our Services, you confirm that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use our Services.

2. Information we collect

We collect information in three main ways: information you provide directly, information we collect automatically, and information we receive from third parties (for example, payment partners or verification providers).

2.1 Information you provide to us

  • Account information: Name, email address, password (encrypted), phone number, and country of residence.
  • Profile information: Date of birth, gender (if you choose to provide it), address, and emergency contact.
  • Application data: Program type (Work & Pay, Express, visa, flights, orientation), preferred country, role, work experience summaries, education, and additional notes you share when applying.
  • Supporting documentation: Where required, you may upload copies or details of your passport, national ID, medicals, police report, and other supporting documents. We instruct you clearly on what is required per program and jurisdiction.
  • Payment information: We collect transaction details such as payment amounts, currency, payment reference, and payment status. Card details or MoMo PINs are processed by our payment partners and are not stored on our servers.
  • Communications: Messages you send using contact forms, support tickets, in-app feedback, or email.

2.2 Information collected automatically

When you use our website or dashboards, we may automatically collect:

  • Usage data: Pages you visit, buttons you click, referring URLs, timestamps, and basic interaction flows.
  • Device and technical data: Browser type, operating system, device type, language preferences, approximate location (from IP), and basic diagnostic logs.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: See Section 6 for more details on our cookie approach.

2.3 Information from third parties

In some cases, we may receive limited information from:

  • Payment providers: Confirmation of payment success, failure, or chargeback status.
  • Verification partners: Confirmation of document validity or screening results, where you have consented to such checks.
  • Referral or partner agencies: Basic information when you are referred to us through a partner, in line with the purpose of your application.

3. How we use your information

We use your information to run Elite Travel & Tour in a safe, responsible, and transparent way. This includes:

  • Service delivery: Processing your applications, bookings, appointments, and payments.
  • Eligibility and program matching: Reviewing your application details to determine whether you meet requirements for specific roles, routes, or services.
  • Account and security: Creating and managing your user account, preventing fraud, and protecting access to your data.
  • Communication: Sending you confirmations, updates, reminders, and important announcements about your application, payments, or program changes.
  • Support and dispute resolution: Addressing your questions, handling complaints, and investigating issues you raise.
  • Improvement and analytics: Understanding how users interact with our website and tools so we can improve clarity, reduce friction, and build better features.
  • Legal and compliance: Complying with applicable laws, regulations, and partner agreements, including record-keeping obligations and risk checks.

Where required by law, we will only process your personal data when we have a valid legal basis to do so (for example: your consent, our contractual obligations to you, our legitimate interests, or compliance with legal obligations).

Important: We do not fabricate documents, bank statements, employment letters, or any other materials. We use your information strictly to support genuine applications and journeys.

4. How we share your information

We do not sell your personal data. We only share information where it is necessary, proportionate, and consistent with this Privacy Policy.

4.1 Service providers and partners

We may share limited data with:

  • Technology providers: Hosting, database, email delivery, analytics, and security tools that help us run and protect the platform.
  • Payment processors: To process payments, verify transactions, and handle disputes or chargebacks.
  • Program and employer partners: Where necessary to assess your suitability, create job or program placements, or complete bookings (for example, hotels, tour companies, or logistics partners).

4.2 Legal, compliance, and safety

We may disclose information where we believe it is reasonably necessary to:

  • Comply with laws, regulations, or valid legal processes.
  • Respond to lawful requests from public authorities.
  • Protect the rights, property, or safety of Elite Travel & Tour, our users, partners, or the public.

4.3 Business transitions

If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, funding round, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction, always subject to this Privacy Policy or an equivalent protection framework.

5. Data retention

We keep your information only for as long as it is needed for the purposes described in this Policy, or as long as required by law or our contractual obligations.

  • Account and profile data: Retained while your account is active. If you request deletion, we will remove or anonymise data unless we must keep it for legal or legitimate business reasons.
  • Application and program records: Kept for a period necessary to manage your journey, handle disputes, and meet legal/regulatory obligations (for example, bookkeeping or compliance audits).
  • Payment records: Maintained for the period required by tax, accounting, and financial regulations.
  • Technical logs: Retained for a limited period and used for security, performance monitoring, and troubleshooting.

When we no longer need your data, we will securely delete it or convert it into aggregated, non-identifiable information.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. We use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Keep you signed in and secure your session.
  • Remember your preferences (such as language or last-selected filters).
  • Understand aggregate usage patterns and improve the experience.

We typically group cookies into:

  • Strictly necessary cookies: Required for authentication, security, and core site functions.
  • Functional cookies: Improve usability, such as remembering preferences or pre-filling forms.
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how users interact with the platform at an aggregate level. We do not use analytics to track or profile individual users for advertising.

Most browsers allow you to control cookies via settings. Disabling certain cookies may impact functionality such as login, tracking, or payment flows.

7. How we protect your information

Security mindset: We design our admin and user dashboards with access controls, audit trails, and clear separation between public and private data.

We implement technical and organisational measures, including:

  • Role-based access controls and per-user isolation for sensitive records where technically supported.
  • Use of reputable cloud infrastructure and security best practices.
  • Session-based authentication with the ability to revoke or sign out.
  • Periodic reviews of database rules and admin flows to minimise privilege escalation risks.

However, no online system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. You are responsible for keeping your login credentials confidential and notifying us promptly if you suspect unauthorised access to your account.

8. Your rights and choices

Depending on your location and applicable laws, you may have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: Request that we delete your data, subject to legal or legitimate retention obligations.
  • Restriction: Ask us to limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: Object to processing based on our legitimate interests, where applicable.
  • Consent withdrawal: Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on your consent.

To exercise your rights, you can contact us using the details in Section 12. We may need to verify your identity before processing certain requests.

9. Accounts, tracking IDs, and communications

When you create an account or submit an application, we generate internal references and, when applicable, tracking IDs to help you follow your journey.

  • Account communications: We send transactional emails and alerts (for example, login notices, application updates, payment confirmations, and important program changes).
  • Tracking updates: Where you have a tracking ID, we use it to surface your case status and related updates in your dashboard or on dedicated tracking pages.
  • Marketing communications: We only send marketing content (such as newsletters or new programmes) where allowed by law and where you have not opted out. You can unsubscribe at any time.
Control: You can review and update key parts of your profile and application details via your account pages. For deeper changes or corrections, contact support.

10. International transfers

Because we connect you to opportunities and partners in different countries, your data may be accessed from or processed in jurisdictions outside your home country.

Where we share data with partners or service providers in other countries, we take steps to ensure an appropriate level of protection for your information, for example through contractual safeguards and limiting the data shared to what is necessary for the specific purpose.

11. Children and minors

Our Services are primarily designed for adults who can enter into travel, employment, or service agreements. Where we work with younger applicants (for example, recent graduates or students), additional parental or guardian consent may be required under local law.

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children without appropriate consent. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data without proper consent, please contact us so we can review and act appropriately.

12. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our Services, legal requirements, or operational practices. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.
  • Provide additional notice within the website or dashboard where appropriate.

Your continued use of the Services after a revised version becomes effective means you accept the updated Policy.

13. How to contact us

If you have questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, you can reach us at:

  • Email: info@elitetravel.com
  • Phone: +233 59 911 7007
  • Office: Elite Travel & Tour, Tema, Greater Accra, Ghana

When contacting us about your data, please do not include sensitive documents (such as full passport scans) unless we have specifically requested them through a secure channel for a defined purpose.
